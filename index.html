RUNWAY MODELS TURNED OUT FOR DAY ONE CASTING

Energy and anticipation filled Work of Heart Studio on J Street as DMR Productions kicked off the opening day of castings for Fashion Week Sacramento 2026. Dozens of aspiring and professional models lined up early, armed with headshots and personality. Producers praised the talent and diversity on display. Event director Melissa Kay stated, “This year we see more representation and creativity than ever before — Sacramento is stepping firmly onto the fashion map.” 

The judging panel — featuring local designers, stylists, and scouting agents — evaluated walks, poses, and presentation. Those selected advance to training workshops ahead of the March show at the California State Railroad Museum. Organizers say their mission is to blend regional culture with style innovation.
FASHION WEEK RETURNS MARCH 14 AT THE RAILROAD MUSEUM Celebrating its 21st edition, Fashion Week Sacramento will transform the California State Railroad Museum into a runway showcase merging historic ambience and modern craft. Headliners include DMR Productions’ signature design segments and collaborations with emerging West Coast brands.
Next Casting January 24 at The Atrium DMR Productions announces a second open call for new faces at The Atrium Gallery in Old Sacramento. The event encourages walk‑ins and registered talents to update their profiles and new headshots on‑site.
Local Designers Reveal Bold Concept Pieces Creative director Nadia Lopez previewed experimental looks featuring recycled textiles and digital prints. “Sacramento fashion is positioning itself as a hub for sustainable design,” she said during the preview.
Behind the Scenes with DMR Productions Months of planning go into casting, music, and lighting. The team credits its streamlined registration system that collects model profiles, measurements, and z‑cards for designer review in minutes.
