RUNWAY MODELS BRING ENERGY TO FIRST CASTING

Main Photo Placeholder — replace with casting image
The opening day of Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 began early at DMR Productions’ Work of Heart Studio. By 7 a.m., more than a hundred models waited outside to take part in the city’s biggest fashion casting yet. Threads of Innovation, this year’s theme, highlights eco‑forward design and racial diversity. Organizers said the event marks a record number of first‑time participants and an expanded schedule of workshops between January and February. 

“We’re seeing talent from almost every county in California this time,” casting director Melissa Kay shared. “Each year brings new faces and connections that push us forward.”  Designers and scouting agents praised DMR for creating a pipeline that links emerging models directly with independent brands. Rehearsals for the March 14 runway show begin next month at the California State Railroad Museum.
FASHION WEEK RETURNS MARCH 14 AT THE RAILROAD MUSEUM Sacramento’s 21st annual Fashion Week marks a milestone for California’s creative community. DMR Productions announced a three‑day festival celebrating the intersection of design, music, and sustainability. More than 30 regional labels will present capsule runs featuring upcycled materials and AI‑assisted pattern drafting. 

Guests can expect interactive installations throughout Old Sacramento. Saturday’s main event culminates with an outdoor “Runway Under the Stars” showcase.

Second Casting Event Announced for January 24 DMR Productions will host another open audition next week at Old Sacramento’s Atrium Gallery. Coordinators expect nearly 500 attendees ranging from returning favorites to new faces registered through the digital portal. Selected participants join the official model roster for Fashion Week Sacramento 2026.
Designers Reveal Preview Collections Showcasing materials made from fruit‑based fibers and repurposed fabrics, local designers Nadia Lopez and Victor Reyes are earning buzz on social media for pushing eco‑fashion boundaries. Their garments blend performance tech with Sacramento’s streetwear culture.
Behind the Scenes with Technical Crew In the weeks before the March premiere, DMR’s technical team fine‑tunes lighting and sound design to align with each collection’s music and theme. Stage manager Lina Nguyen said the goal is to make every transition feel cinematic for viewers and guests watching online.
