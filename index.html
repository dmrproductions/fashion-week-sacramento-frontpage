RUNWAY MODELS BRING ENERGY TO FIRST CASTING

Main Photo Placeholder – replace with casting image
Inside Work of Heart Studio on J Street, an eager crowd of hopeful models lined up for the first day of Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 castings. 

DMR Productions team members reviewed applications and previewed runway walks throughout the morning. Creative director James Porter shared:  “We’ve never seen such diversity and confidence come through our doors.” 

This year’s theme, ‘Threads of Innovation,’ focuses on sustainable textiles and inclusive representation. Final selections will advance into training sessions curated by industry mentors ahead of the March runway premiere at the California State Railroad Museum.  Organizers say the move underscores Sacramento’s rise as a cultural fashion hub.
FASHION WEEK RETURNS MARCH 14 AT THE RAILROAD MUSEUM Mark your calendars for a three‑day celebration of style, art, and community at one of California’s most distinctive venues.  Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 will open its runway on March 14 featuring a hybrid of in‑person and live‑streamed collections from regional brands.  Attendees can expect 40+ looks per show and exclusive after‑events in Old Town.

Second Casting Announced for January 24 DMR Productions confirms a follow‑up casting at Old Sacramento’s Atrium Gallery on January 24. Participants can register online and receive professional comp card updates through the organization’s model portal.
Local Designers Preview Eco‑Focused Lines Designers Nadia Lopez and Victor Reyes will integrate plant‑based fibers and repurposed materials into their FW26 collections, accelerating the city’s shift toward sustainable fashion economies.
Behind the Scenes with Production Crew Lighting engineer Miguel Ramos and his team spend weeks programming transitions for each segment. “Precision is everything — we choreograph music and spotlights like a dance,” Ramos explained.
