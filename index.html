RUNWAY STARS DISCOVERED AT MORNING CASTING

Record turnout brings fresh faces and fierce energy to Fashion Week Sacramento 2026

Live Casting Photo Goes Here
SPOTLIGHT MOMENT: Selected models showcase their runway presence during today's casting at DMR Productions' Work of Heart Studio. Featured talent advances to Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 training workshops.

The energy was electric at Work of Heart Studio this morning as Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 launched its most competitive casting season yet. Before dawn broke over the capital, more than two hundred aspiring and professional models had gathered outside DMR Productions' headquarters, ready to compete for coveted spots on the March 14th runway.

This year's theme, "Threads of Innovation," demands models who can embody both high-fashion sophistication and the authentic street culture that defines Sacramento's creative renaissance. Casting director Melissa Kay noted the exceptional diversity in today's turnout, with participants ranging from seasoned professionals to first-time hopefuls discovering their runway potential.

"What we're seeing today goes beyond traditional beauty standards," Kay explained as models cycled through their walks. "We're looking for storytellers—people who can transform fabric into narrative and make audiences feel something real."

The selection process combines technical evaluation with artistic vision. Each model performs a signature walk, poses for digital headshots, and participates in brief interviews with the creative team. Selected participants will join an intensive training program throughout February, preparing for the Railroad Museum showcase that promises to redefine California fashion events.

Executive producer Duane Ram, whose Emmy-recognized background in videography shapes the event's cinematic approach, emphasized the career-building aspect of participation. "Fashion Week Sacramento isn't just one night—it's a launchpad. Models who walk our runway consistently book campaigns, editorials, and opportunities throughout the West Coast."

Today's casting represents the first of two selection opportunities, with the final call scheduled for next week at Old Sacramento's Atrium Gallery. For those featured in today's session, the journey toward March 14th has officially begun. For those still preparing, next Saturday offers another chance to join Sacramento's most prestigious fashion showcase.

FASHION WEEK RETURNS MARCH 14 AT RAILROAD MUSEUM Sacramento's 21st annual Fashion Week transforms the historic California State Railroad Museum into a cutting-edge fashion showcase featuring over thirty regional designers. The three-day celebration highlights sustainable innovation, cultural storytelling, and the intersection of technology with traditional craftsmanship.

This year's expanded program includes designer workshops, sustainability forums, and interactive installations throughout Old Sacramento. The weekend culminates with Saturday's "Runway Under the Stars" outdoor showcase, where selected models will present collections against the backdrop of the capital's skyline.

Tickets go on sale February 1st through DMR Productions, with VIP packages offering backstage access, designer meet-and-greets, and exclusive after-party invitations. Early bird pricing available for the first 48 hours.

Final Casting Call: January 24 at Atrium Gallery

DMR Productions announces the second and final casting opportunity for Fashion Week Sacramento 2026. The session takes place next Saturday at Old Sacramento's Atrium Gallery, providing additional chances for models who missed today's event or seek callbacks from the initial round. Registration opens at 9 AM with walk-ins welcomed until 4 PM. Participants should arrive in fitted black attire with minimal accessories, ready for digital portfolio updates and runway evaluation. Selected models join the official roster for March's showcase and gain access to exclusive training workshops throughout February. The historic gallery space will be configured as a temporary runway for the day-long selection process, allowing casting directors to evaluate talent in a professional environment that mirrors the actual show venue atmosphere.

Work of Heart Studio: Where Dreams Take Flight

Today's casting venue, Work of Heart Studio, continues its partnership with Fashion Week Sacramento as both location sponsor and creative hub. Owner Sarah Chen has transformed the historic Old Sacramento building into a premier destination for fashion photography, videography, and industry events. "Supporting Fashion Week aligns with our mission to elevate local talent," Chen explained during this morning's setup. The studio's exposed brick walls, professional lighting systems, and spacious layout make it ideal for casting sessions and portfolio development. Throughout the year, Work of Heart hosts designer workshops, model training sessions, and collaborative projects that strengthen Sacramento's fashion ecosystem. The venue's commitment to providing accessible creative space has helped launch dozens of careers in modeling, design, and fashion photography, making it an integral part of the capital's creative renaissance.

Emmy-Winning Production Standards

Fashion Week Sacramento's technical excellence stems from producer Duane Ram's award-winning background in videography and live event production. The Emmy-recognized team approaches each casting and runway show with broadcast-quality standards, ensuring that digital audiences receive the same immersive experience as front-row guests. Advanced lighting design, multi-camera coverage, and precision audio engineering transform fashion presentations into cinematic experiences. This technical sophistication has helped establish Fashion Week Sacramento as a legitimate industry event that attracts national attention and provides participating models with professional-grade portfolio content. The production team's attention to detail extends from pre-event planning through post-show digital distribution, creating a comprehensive media package that benefits designers, models, and sponsors alike while elevating Sacramento's reputation as a serious fashion destination.
