SPOTLIGHT MOMENT: Models showcase their runway presence at the model castings for Fashion Week Sacramento

The energy was electric at Work of Heart Studio this morning as Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 launched its most competitive casting season yet. Before dawn broke over the capital, more than two hundred aspiring and professional models had gathered outside, ready to compete for coveted spots on the March 14th runway.

This year's theme, "Fashion Reimagined‚ÄîBlending Nature, Technology, and Style," demands models who can embody both high-fashion sophistication and the authentic street culture that defines Sacramento's creative renaissance. Casting Assistant Nikisha Mitran noted the exceptional diversity in today's turnout, with participants ranging from seasoned professionals to first-time hopefuls discovering their runway potential.

"What we're seeing today goes beyond traditional beauty standards," Mitran explained as models cycled through their walks. "We're looking for models who can not only walk but transform themselves into the designer's vision and help tell their story."

The selection process combines technical evaluation with artistic vision. Each model poses for digital headshots and performs a basic walk followed by a more dramatic presentation with attitude for the designers in attendance.

Day Two Model Casting: January 24 at Atrium Gallery

Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 holds its final casting session Saturday, January 24, at Old Sacramento's Atrium Gallery. The casting runs from 11 AM to 2 PM with walk-ins welcome. Models should wear fitted black attire with minimal accessories for digital portfolio shots and runway evaluation. You don't need to have an agent or prior experience. Selected participants receive spots on the March 14th runway.

Regalia in Bloom Presented as The Regalia Chamber

Regalia in Bloom, photographer Trin De's ongoing exploration of adornment as authority, presents an installation at Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 through The Regalia Chamber, a site-specific exhibition which draws from the Regalia Archives. The installation showcases De's ability to create evocative scenes while presenting samples of the extraordinary wardrobe available for commissioned photography work.

Two Decades of Fashion in the Capital

For two decades, DMR Productions has transformed Fashion Week Sacramento into a collaborative platform that unites the region's creative community. Producer Duane Ram's commitment to nurturing emerging talent extends through ongoing partnerships with academic fashion programs at Sacramento State and Delta College, providing students real-world runway experience alongside established professionals.
