Fashion Week Sacramento 2026 kicked off Saturday as DMR Productions hosted its first open casting at Work of Heart Studio on J Street. The crowd was electric as hundreds of hopefuls posed before industry judges. Casting director Melissa Kay explained that more than 60% of this year’s registrants were new talent. 

Photographers, stylists, and designers scouted the room for fresh faces and unique looks. Final selections advance to training workshops and rehearsals for the March 14 runway premiere at the California State Railroad Museum. “This city has talent ready for the world,” Kay added, as attendees cheered.
FASHION WEEK RETURNS MARCH 14 AT THE RAILROAD MUSEUM Now in its 21st year, Fashion Week Sacramento continues to shine as a hub of style and innovation. The 2026 edition combines heritage ambience with sustainable design on the runway, spotlighting regional creators and Green Fashion initiatives.

Next Casting January 24 at The Atrium DMR Productions has announced a second casting event at Old Sacramento’s Atrium Gallery on January 24. Applicants will be considered for feature segments during the main show. Pros and newcomers alike are encouraged to bring professional headshots and a one‑minute walk.
Local Designers Push Creative Boundaries Sacramento’s Nadia Lopez and Lara Keane revealed sneak peeks of their upcoming lines, fusing reclaimed materials and technological textiles. Their collections aim to put Sacramento alongside Los Angeles and Portland as a west coast design capital.
Behind the Scenes with DMR Productions Preparation for Fashion Week starts months in advance. From venue logistics to designer coordination, DMR’s crew manages nearly 100 staff roles covering lighting, sound, and casting operations across venues.
